1. God rebukes Samuel's mourning over Saul and sends him to Bethlehem with a mission to anoint the next king (v. 1).
   - God's plan for Israel's king was never undone by Saul's failure.
1. Samuel fears Saul will kill him, and God provides a cover by telling him to bring a heifer for sacrifice (v. 2).
   - This is not God commanding a lie, for He does desire that Samuel do the sacrifice. It is a partial disclosure
1. Samuel arrives and immediately repeats Israel's mistake, by assuming the man who seems outwardly "king-like" is the intended anointee.
   - God rebukes Samuel for this, "the LORD does not see as man does. For man sees the outward
   appearance, but the LORD sees the heart" (v. 7).
1. The chosen one is out tending the sheep (v. 11), not where one would naturally look for a king.
1. Samuel anoints David, and immediately the Spirit of the LORD rushed upon David (v. 13).
   - The Spirit departs from Saul and a spirit of distress is introduced (v. 14).
   - David enters Saul's service as a musician and armor-bearer (vv. 21–22), unbeknownst to Saul that David is the anointed replacement.
1. Like Christ, David comes from unexpected circumstances, and will not be revealed until the time is right.
